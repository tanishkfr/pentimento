# THEORY.md

## Pentimento Conceptual Framework

**Version:** 0.1

**Status:** Working Theory

This document defines the conceptual framework behind Pentimento.

It is not a product specification.

It is a model for thinking about cultural evolution.

Every concept in this document is provisional until supported by evidence.

The purpose of this document is to create a shared language for reasoning about the project.

---

# The Core Proposition

Current digital products preserve cultural consumption.

This project investigates whether interactive systems can instead preserve and explain cultural evolution — and, since the v0.2 reframe, what a person needs when a system attempts that explanation: evidence, honest uncertainty, and a right of reply.

The goal is not to understand media.

The goal is to understand people through their relationship with media — with the person holding final authority over the account.

---

# The Reframe (v0.2)

The primary contribution of this framework is no longer the description of drift. It is the **interaction grammar for contestable machine narratives**:

Propose → Show evidence → Accept, Reframe, or Strike → Correction outranks reading → Revision carries the scar.

The four layers below remain as working scaffolding for the movie-archive domain. They are instruments, not the contribution. Treat them accordingly.

---

# The Theory of Cultural Drift

Human cultural preferences do not appear instantly.

They emerge gradually through repeated exploration, reflection, social influence, and lived experience.

This gradual process is referred to throughout this project as **Cultural Drift**.

The system does not attempt to predict drift.

It attempts to make drift observable.

---

# The Four Layers

The project distinguishes four different layers.

These layers should never be confused.

---

## Layer 1

### Behavior

Observable actions.

Examples:

* Watched a movie
* Saved a film
* Rewatched
* Reviewed
* Followed a director

Behavior is factual.

The system should never speculate about behavior.

---

## Layer 2

### Exploration

A temporary period of focused curiosity.

Examples:

* Korean cinema
* Slow cinema
* Psychological thrillers
* Humanist dramas

Explorations begin.

Explorations evolve.

Explorations fade.

Explorations may overlap.

An exploration is not permanent.

---

## Layer 3

### Taste

Long-term recurring patterns.

Unlike explorations, taste persists.

Examples:

* Preference for ambiguity
* Preference for character-driven stories
* Preference for atmosphere over spectacle

Taste emerges through repeated explorations.

It should not be inferred from isolated events.

---

## Layer 4

### Identity

The broadest layer.

Identity is not inferred directly.

Instead, it is reflected through long-term cultural behavior.

Pentimento should never claim to know who someone is.

It only helps users investigate recurring patterns.

---

# Cultural Drift

The central phenomenon investigated by this project.

Working definition:

> The gradual evolution of long-term cultural preferences through repeated periods of exploration.

The project visualizes drift.

It does not attempt to stop or optimize it.

---

# Explorations

Explorations are the primary organizational unit.

Current products organize around movies.

Pentimento organizes around explorations.

Characteristics:

* temporary
* curiosity-driven
* loosely bounded
* incomplete
* personal

An exploration may contain:

* movies
* directors
* communities
* essays
* interviews
* discussions

The boundaries are intentionally fuzzy.

---

# Shifts

A Shift is a meaningful change in observable behavior.

Examples:

Watching substantially different kinds of films.

Following new creators.

Beginning a long exploration.

Returning to previously abandoned interests.

Shifts are observable.

Their causes are not.

---

# Interpretation

Interpretations connect evidence into meaning.

Example.

Behavior:

Watched several Korean films over three months.

Interpretation:

"You appear to have entered a sustained exploration of Korean cinema."

Interpretations are hypotheses.

Never facts.

---

# Evidence

Every interpretation must reference observable evidence.

Evidence may include:

Behavior

Time

Frequency

Sequences

Revisits

Communities

Reviews

Notes

Without evidence there is no interpretation.

---

# Uncertainty

Every interpretation has uncertainty.

The system should communicate this honestly.

Good:

"It looks like..."

"We noticed..."

"This may indicate..."

Bad:

"You became..."

"You are..."

"The system knows..."

Confidence should be earned.

Never assumed.

---

# Human + System Collaboration

Pentimento is not an automated interpretation engine.

Understanding emerges through collaboration.

Interaction model:

Behavior

↓

System proposes

↓

Human evaluates

↓

Shared understanding evolves

The human remains the final authority.

---

# The Role of AI

AI is not the product.

AI assists with:

* clustering
* summarization
* explanation
* narrative synthesis

AI should remain invisible whenever possible.

The project should still make conceptual sense if AI were removed.

---

# The Central Loop

Observe

↓

Identify possible patterns

↓

Present evidence

↓

Invite interpretation

↓

Refine understanding

This loop is the heart of Pentimento.

Every feature should support it.

---

# What This Framework Refuses To Do

The system should never:

Assign personality types.

Predict intelligence.

Claim psychological certainty.

Reduce identity to movies.

Replace human reflection.

Produce opaque conclusions.

Every interpretation remains open to revision.

---

# Relationship Between Concepts

Behavior creates evidence.

Evidence reveals explorations.

Repeated explorations shape taste.

Long-term taste reflects identity.

Identity influences future behavior.

This creates a continuous cycle rather than a linear progression.

---

# The Algorithm Confound

The strongest objection to this framework, stated plainly:

> An archive of watched media records what platforms served as much as what a person sought. Observed "drift" may be the recommender's drift, not the viewer's.

The framework does not evade this. It treats provenance as first-class evidence.

A film watched within weeks of release, during a period of calendar-paced viewing, was plausibly *served*. A decades-old film found during a period of archive-diving was plausibly *sought*. The ratio of found to fed — and its movement over time — is itself observable evidence.

What remains unknowable stays unknowable: every "found" film was still surfaced by something. The system may present the found/fed pattern. It must never claim to know who chose.

This turns the framework's largest vulnerability into one of its questions:

> How much of the drift was yours?

The honest answer is always partial. The system should say so.

---

# Why This Matters

Current platforms optimize for prediction.

Pentimento optimizes for understanding.

Prediction answers:

"What will you probably watch?"

Understanding asks:

"What has your journey meant?"

This distinction defines the project.

---

# Success Criteria

The theory succeeds if users naturally recognize themselves in its interpretations.

The theory fails if:

People describe it as another recommendation system.

People cannot distinguish it from existing movie apps.

Interpretations feel arbitrary.

Users cannot understand why conclusions were reached.

The framework should make people feel understood.

Not analyzed.
