# LANGUAGE.md

## Pentimento Shared Vocabulary

**Version:** 0.1

**Status:** Living Document

This document defines the language of Pentimento.

The purpose of this vocabulary is consistency.

Every concept should have exactly one meaning.

Every interface, prototype, case study, and implementation should use these terms consistently.

If two terms describe the same thing, one of them should be removed.

---

# Core Principle

Language shapes thought.

The words used inside Pentimento are not branding.

They define the mental model of the product.

When introducing a new term, ask:

* Does this describe a real human behavior?
* Is it understandable without explanation?
* Does it remove ambiguity?
* Would a user naturally say something similar?

If not, don't invent it.

---

# Exploration

**Definition**

A temporary period of focused curiosity around a cultural topic.

Examples:

* Korean cinema
* Psychological thrillers
* Studio Ghibli
* Noir films
* Humanist dramas

An exploration has:

* a beginning
* momentum
* branching
* natural decline

An exploration is **not** a playlist.

An exploration is **not** a collection.

An exploration represents a period of curiosity.

---

# Cultural Drift

**Definition**

The gradual evolution of long-term cultural preferences through repeated explorations.

Internal shorthand only.

Do not present "Cultural Drift" as a novel construct — sociology has studied taste trajectories for decades (see RESEARCH.md, Positioning). In the product and the case study, prefer plain English: "how taste changes."

Drift is not a goal.

It is an observation.

---

# Strike

The reader's refusal of a reading.

A struck reading is never deleted. The claim stays in the draft, struck through; the retraction is recorded; the evidence remains.

Striking is not an error state.

It is the interaction the whole grammar exists to make possible.

---

# Correction

The reader's own words, offered in place of a struck reading.

Corrections outrank readings.

The system never edits, paraphrases, or improves a correction.

---

# Pentimento (the name)

In painting: an earlier stroke left visible through the finished work — the maker's change of mind, kept. The project is named for the artifact it produces: a revision that never hides what it revised.

---

# Second Draft (the revision)

The revision the system writes after the reader has struck or corrected it.

A second draft must:

* keep the first draft's struck claims visible as scars
* let corrections lead
* never quietly restore a withdrawn reading

The second draft is the proof that the right of reply is real.

---

# Right of Reply

The principle underneath everything.

Software that narrates a person owes that person evidence, honest uncertainty, and the standing to disagree — with the disagreement preserved in the record.

---

# Behavior

Observable actions.

Examples:

* Watched
* Rewatched
* Reviewed
* Saved
* Recommended
* Followed a director

Behavior is factual.

Behavior should never be interpreted without evidence.

---

# Evidence

Observable information supporting an interpretation.

Evidence might include:

* viewing history
* timestamps
* review text
* rewatch patterns
* sequence of discoveries
* frequency
* gaps in viewing

Evidence answers:

> "Why does the system think this?"

Every interpretation must have evidence.

---

# Interpretation

A possible explanation built from evidence.

Interpretations are always hypotheses.

Never facts.

Good examples:

> "It looks like..."

> "This may indicate..."

Poor examples:

> "You became..."

> "Your personality is..."

Interpretations invite discussion.

They do not end it.

---

# Shift

A meaningful change in observable behavior.

Examples:

Beginning an exploration.

Leaving one.

Returning to a forgotten interest.

Changing viewing patterns.

A Shift is detected.

Its cause is not assumed.

---

# Marker

A cultural experience that helps reveal a shift.

Markers do not necessarily cause change.

Sometimes they simply make an existing change visible.

Example:

A user watches *Perfect Days* after months of exploring slower cinema.

The film may reveal an ongoing shift rather than create one.

---

# Catalyst

A cultural experience that appears to initiate sustained exploration.

Unlike a Marker, a Catalyst is followed by measurable behavioral change.

Whether something is truly a Catalyst remains uncertain.

The system should communicate this uncertainty.

---

# Chapter

A user-defined name for an exploration.

Examples:

"The Year Subtitles Disappeared"

"My A24 Obsession"

"Finding Quiet Films"

"The Nolan Rabbit Hole"

Chapters are authored by users.

Explorations are inferred by the system.

These are different concepts.

---

# Curiosity

A temporary desire to explore something unfamiliar.

Curiosity creates explorations.

Curiosity is temporary.

Taste is relatively stable.

The two should never be confused.

---

# Taste

Long-term recurring patterns that emerge from many explorations.

Taste is not inferred from one movie.

Taste is not inferred from one rating.

Taste emerges slowly.

---

# Identity

The broadest concept in the framework.

Pentimento does not attempt to define identity.

It investigates how cultural behavior may reflect parts of identity over time.

Identity is intentionally left open-ended.

---

# Reflection

The process of recognizing meaning in past experiences.

Reflection is the desired outcome.

Not prediction.

Not engagement.

Not optimization.

---

# Sensemaking

The collaborative process of organizing evidence into understanding.

Pentimento is fundamentally a sensemaking interface.

The system contributes observations.

The user contributes context.

Understanding emerges together.

---

# Journey

A term intentionally avoided.

Reason:

A journey implies one continuous path.

Real cultural behavior is branching, cyclical, and incomplete.

Prefer:

* Exploration
* Drift
* Chapter

---

# Trail

A legacy term from the project's first life as "Taste Trails."

Avoid using it entirely.

The project investigates explorations and contested readings, not predefined trails. The old name anchored the project to movies and paths; the rename to Pentimento (RESEARCH.md, E4) retired both the term and the metaphor.

---

# Recommendation

Use carefully.

Pentimento does not primarily recommend media.

It supports ongoing explorations.

Whenever possible, prefer:

* Continue this exploration
* Explore another direction
* Follow this idea

instead of:

* Recommended for you

---

# AI

Avoid presenting AI as an actor.

Never write:

"The AI thinks..."

Prefer:

"We noticed..."

"It looks like..."

"Based on your viewing history..."

The focus should remain on the user's experience rather than the technology.

---

# Words We Prefer

* Explore
* Notice
* Reflect
* Discover
* Connect
* Revisit
* Recognize
* Continue
* Understand

---

# Words We Avoid

* Predict
* Analyze
* Optimize
* Personality
* Score
* Intelligence
* Compatibility
* Accuracy
* Algorithm

These words shift attention toward computation instead of understanding.

---

# Language Principles

Use plain English.

Avoid academic language in the product.

Avoid startup language.

Avoid AI buzzwords.

Every sentence should feel like it came from a thoughtful friend rather than a data dashboard.

The product should sound curious.

Never certain.

Never authoritative.

Always collaborative.
